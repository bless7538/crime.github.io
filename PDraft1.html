What is Crime?




Crime is an unlawful act punishable by a state or other authority.
This would be a normal oxford dictionary meaning of crime but it doesn't particularly define it.
Crime is actually a means in which people take advantage of others in many ways which is illegal but can cause harm to one or even death along with causing suffering to the family, friends and acquaintances of the victim.


Crimes can include:

  1. Stealing



  2. Murder



  3. Human Trafficking


Factors Which Can Cause People To Commit Crimes


For some people, crime is a way for them to escape the problems and challenges that they were facing in life.
But for others, it was a way for them to make money easily without having to work for it.
Even teenagers engage in crime due to peer pressure or because of influence of others in their community, while others break the law because of alchol or drug abuse. People who engage in crime can be anyone of any age, size or ethnicty.
Even in Trinidad and Tobago there are many young people engaging in criminal activities that cause them to drop school, involve themselves in gangs or worse, even die.




How Does Crime Affect a Country


  • It can cause a reduction in the population of a country
  • It reduces the workforce and revenue in the country
  • Crime can scare tourists
  • It can affect productivity
